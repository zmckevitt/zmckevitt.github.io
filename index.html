About Me

Overview

Research based Masters computer science student at the University of Colorado Boulder graduating Spring 2023. I am currently a researcher in the Computer Architecture group under Professor Tamara Lehman where my research is on finite state machine formalization for speculative execution attacks (Spectre). Over the summer, I spent my time as a systems software engineering intern at VMware making changes to the ESX hypervisor.

My primary research interests lie broadly in computer systems and security and related applications in cryptography and privacy. In addition to hardware security, I am interested in various problems in security and applied cryptography as they relate to operating systems, networks, and distributed systems. In the near future, I plan on pursuing a PhD in computer systems security where I hope to expand my research skills beyond computer architecture to include other systems areas, and I aim to focus on privacy related issues. Through my positions as a grader and TA, I have also come to thoroughly enjoy teaching, and I am excited to pursue this passion more during my PhD studies.

In my free time, I like to ski, read, and travel!

About Me

Overview

Masters computer science student at the University of Colorado Boulder. I am a researcher in the Electrical, Computer, and Energy Engineering (ECEE) department as a member of the computer architecture group where my research is on using deep learning techniques to identify C programs that may be vulnerable to Spectre attacks. I am currently at VMware.

My primary interests lie broadly in computer systems and security, although I have interests in cryptography, compilers, and theory as well. I am most experienced with the C programming language and have developed systems code in domains such as operating systems, networked applications, and distributed systems. In the security domain, I am familiar with various C/Linux exploits (buffer overflows, ROP, off-by-one, etc.) and have experience analyzing x86 Assembly using debuggers like GDB. Due to my research, I am also knowledgable of transient execution vulnerabilities/side-channel attacks and how they exploit various microarchitectures.

In my free time, I like to ski, read, and play tennis! A few of my favorite books are the Brothers Karamazov, Sapiens, Island (Aldous Huxley), and Siddhartha.
